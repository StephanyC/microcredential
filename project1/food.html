Dominican Traditional Food

    Dominican Breakfast

  • Mangu:mashed plantains with added butter and water.
  • Salami:pork meat that is fried with oil.
  • Cheese:diary that is fried typically the tropical brand.

    Dominican Lunch: La Bandera

    This is known as the dominican national lunch meal. Rice, Chicken, and
    are the ingredients of the meal.

  • Rice:you can use either white rice or a combination of beans and rice.
  • Chicken: Braised Chicken. Brand does not matter.
  • Beans:Boiled water then seasoned. You can use red beans and pinto beans.

    Dominican Dessert: Habichuela Con Dulce

    This is a sweet bean dessert that is commonly eaten during easter.
    Raisins are optional.

  • Red Beans:Goya or another brand
  • Sweet Potato:Root vegetables that are sold in the grocery store
  • Coconut Milk:Goya brand or another brand
